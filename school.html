Back to School

It's almost that time again this year. We are sending our children back to school. Yet, this year is very different. We've been through a lot in the last school year. Some parents have seen their children go off to school as usual but in masks and heavily stocked with hand sanitizer and prayers. Some parents have helped their children navigate homeschool and/or school online. New obstacles have presented themselves. New challenges have come our way. As we transition back to school for the new school year, I hope this devotion and activity will inspire hope within all of us.

A review of 2020…

  • March 2020
    After months of listening to reports from the CDC, a lockdown began nation-wide. We left our jobs to work at home. We left our schools to study from home. And we closed all our businesses that were not vital.
  • April 2020
    We thought it would only last a few weeks. We kept hearing two weeks at most. Many of us were heartbroken to watch as things got worse and we needed to continue to stay at home. Many jobs became work-from-home permanently and many parents decided their children would not return to school to finish the school year.
  • Summer 2020
    The summer loomed before us, kids at home, parents at home, pools and summer camps closed. Graduations were online and no one was sure exactly what the 2021 school year was going to look like.
  • Fall 2020 to Present
    A new school year started and hard decisions were made. We spent the last year making those decisions over and over, watching and waiting, praying and hoping. We were grateful when vaccines became available. We were saddened by the arrival of the Delta variant. This is where we find ourselves.

In Paul's letter to the Philippians…

Paul thanks the Philippians for their friendship, love, and prayers. He reminds them — keep praying. All prayers are heard by God and God understands us in ways we cannot know. God will comfort us and set our hearts at ease. We can find a strength in Christ we had no idea we had. Paul is also expressing joy that this community has been concerned for him. When we pray for others we are showing Christ's love -- even if we don't know someone personally. We too can be prayed for and it enhances our relationship with God and God's people to pray for one another.

Dear God,

Hear our prayers for your Spirit to bring about new ideas in our students and teachers as they return to school. Inspire us to be innovative and creative in all we do. Give us new energy each day to work hard and when we feel weary, comfort us and remind us of your presence.

Amen.

Dear God,

We pray today for open minds ready to learn. Help students learn new things as they begin a new year. Help teachers learn from their students. Help us all to be life-long learners and know we can always learn and grow in our faith even when our school days have ended.

Amen.

Dear God,

Be with our students as we send them out into the world to learn. Keep them safe. Open their minds to new and different ideas. Inspire them and help them find joy in each day, even when their studies are difficult.

Amen.

Dear God,

Hear our prayers for our teachers. Pour out your blessings upon them because they are charged to do your work in the lives of our children. Help us to remember to thank them, be kind to them, and appreciate them for all they do. When they are tired and weary, give them strength.

Amen.

Dear God,

As our children, friends, and teachers return to school we pray they will find friendship among their peers. Help students and children reach out to those who might need friends. Inspire each of us to love our neighbors as you have loved us and embrace everyone we meet.

Amen.

Dear God,

As this new school year begins, fill our hearts with love. Help us find a love for learning. Remind us to love one another as you have loved us. Help our teachers love our students. Help our students love our teachers. Fill our schools with love.

Amen.

Dear God,

Today we pray for growth. We pray that our hearts will expand and our minds will be open to grow in knowledge. As each day goes by throughout this school year help us not only physically grow but grow in faith, knowledge, and love.

Amen.

Dear God,

This last year has changed the way we hear the word 'safe.' Today we pray our students and teachers might be safe and healthy as this school year begins. We pray you will continue to protect us and help us make safe decisions. We pray for the safety of all.

Amen.
